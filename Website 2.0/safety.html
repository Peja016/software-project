Cycling Safety in Dublin

Your safety is our top priority at Dublin Bike Share. Whether you're a daily commuter or occasional rider, following proper cycling practices ensures a positive experience for everyone. Here are important safety guidelines to remember when using our bike share service throughout Dublin.

1 Protective Gear

While helmets are not provided with our bikes, we strongly encourage all riders to wear their own helmet. According to safety statistics, wearing a helmet reduces the risk of head injury by up to 70%.

Additional recommended protective items include:

  • Reflective clothing, especially during low-light conditions
  • Gloves for better grip and hand protection
  • Appropriate footwear that won't slip off pedals

2 Before You Ride

Always inspect your bike before setting off:

  • Check that brakes function properly
  • Ensure tires are properly inflated
  • Test that the handlebars and seat are securely fastened
  • Make sure the chain runs smoothly
  • Verify both front and rear lights are working

If you find any issues with your bike, return it to the dock and select another one. Report any problems through our app.

3 Traffic Rules

Cyclists must follow the same traffic rules as other vehicles:

  • Obey all traffic lights and stop signs
  • Ride in the same direction as traffic, never against it
  • Use hand signals to indicate turns and stops
  • Yield to pedestrians at crosswalks
  • Stop at red lights and stop signs

4 Luas Tracks Caution

Dublin's tram tracks present unique hazards for cyclists:

  • Cross tracks at a 90-degree angle whenever possible
  • Never ride alongside or parallel to tracks
  • Take extra care when tracks are wet, as they become extremely slippery
  • Be aware that bicycle wheels can get caught in the track grooves

5 Cycling Lanes

Dublin has an expanding network of cycling infrastructure:

  • Use dedicated cycle lanes whenever available
  • On streets without cycle lanes, maintain a position about 1 meter from the curb
  • Be aware of bus lanes ‚Äì cyclists are permitted to use most bus lanes during their hours of operation
  • Watch for opening car doors when cycling alongside parked vehicles

6 Weather Conditions

Dublin's variable weather requires adaptability:

  • Rain: Brake earlier as stopping distances increase on wet surfaces
  • Wind: Be prepared for strong gusts, especially on bridges and open areas
  • Fog: Use lights and reflective gear for maximum visibility
  • Adjust your riding style according to conditions ‚Äì slow down when necessary

Night Cycling

All Dublin Bike Share bicycles are equipped with front and rear lights that activate automatically. However, to enhance your visibility:

  • Wear bright, reflective clothing
  • Consider additional clip-on lights for extra visibility
  • Choose well-lit routes when possible
  • Make eye contact with drivers to ensure they've seen you
  • Be extra cautious at intersections

Remember ‚Äì Safety is a Shared Responsibility

By cycling safely and responsibly, you not only protect yourself but also contribute to making Dublin's streets safer for everyone. Stay alert, respect other road users, and enjoy your ride!
